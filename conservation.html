Conservation at Our Zoo

At our zoo, conservation is at the heart of everything we do. We are committed to protecting endangered species and preserving wildlife habitats. Our efforts in Africa, in collaboration with local communities and international partners, have made a significant impact in protecting some of the most vulnerable species. Join us in our mission to safeguard the biodiversity of our planet for future generations.

African Lion

Through habitat restoration and anti-poaching efforts, our zoo has contributed to the growing population of African lions. We work with reserves in Africa to ensure these magnificent animals thrive.

African Elephant

Our team has been actively involved in protecting African elephants from illegal poaching for ivory. By supporting wildlife rangers and local communities, we ensure their protection in the wild.

Black Rhinoceros

With less than 5,000 individuals left in the wild, black rhinos are critically endangered. Our zoo's breeding and conservation programs in Africa help maintain genetic diversity and prevent extinction.
